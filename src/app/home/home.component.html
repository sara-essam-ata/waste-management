Waste deffinition

Waste is the total remnants of human domestic, agricultural, industrial and production activities, that is, all waste left in a place, the neglect of which threatens and harms public health and safety. Waste is divided into several types in terms of its danger or quality, including: • Benign waste: It is the sum of materials whose presence does not pose serious environmental problems, and which are easy to dispose of in an environmentally safe manner. • Hazardous waste: Those whose components contain metallic or radioactive compounds that lead to serious environmental problems. These hazardous wastes are generated from industrial and chemical materials and waste, and agricultural waste (chemical materials that are used as fortifiers in agriculture). Waste Management

Waste management is all the necessary steps to reduce the harmful effects on animals, humans, and nature, and includes waste collection, transportation, treatment, disposal methods, responsible recycling, and control of all garbage that results from human activity.
